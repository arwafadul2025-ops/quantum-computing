

1. Which of the following best describes the phenomenon of superposition in
quantum computing? a) A qubit can exist in multiple states simultaneously until
measured. b) A qubit can only be in a state of 0 or 1 at any given time. c) Two
qubits are linked in such a way that they share the same fate. d) The process of
measuring a qubit’s state
The correct answer is a) A qubit can exist in multiple states simultaneously until measured.




2. What is the primary purpose of a Hadamard gate when applied to a qubit initially

in the ∣0⟩ state? a) To flip the qubit from ∣0⟩ to ∣1⟩. b) To entangle two qubits. c) To

put the qubit into a superposition of ∣0⟩ and ∣1⟩. d) To measure the qubit’s state.

The correct answer is c) To put the qubit into a superposition of |0\rangle and |1\rangle.




3. If a quantum system has n qubits, how many complex amplitudes are needed to

fully describe its state? a) n b) 2n c) n

2 d) 2^n

The correct answer is d) 2^n.

4. Which quantum algorithm offers a quadratic speedup for searching an unsorted

database compared to classical algorithms? a) Shor’s Algorithm b) Deutsch-Jozsa

Algorithm c) Grover’s Algorithm d) Quantum Phase Estimation

The correct answer is c) Grover’s Algorithm.

5. What is the main challenge that quantum error correction aims to address? a)

The difficulty of building large quantum computers. b) The fragility of qubits due

to environmental noise and decoherence. c) The exponential growth of

computational resources needed for quantum simulation. d) The lack of suitable
quantum programming languages

The correct answer is b) The fragility of qubits due to environmental noise and decoherence.

6.Which of the following is a key characteristic of superconducting qubits? a) They

operate at room temperature. b) They require extremely low temperatures

(millikelvins). c) They use individual atoms suspended in electromagnetic fields.

d) They are inherently robust against local errors due to topological properties


the correct answer is b) They require extremely low temperatures (millikelvins).

7. What is the primary threat that Shor’s algorithm poses to current cryptographic

systems? a) It can break symmetric encryption algorithms like AES. b) It can

efficiently factor large numbers, compromising RSA and ECC. c) It can solve the
traveling salesman problem much faster. d) It can accelerate machine learning
tasks, leading to privacy breaches.
The correct answer is b) It can efficiently factor large numbers, compromising RSA and ECC.

8. Which of the following is a hybrid quantum-classical algorithm used to find the
ground state energy of molecules? a) Grover’s Algorithm b) Quantum Phase
Estimation (QPE) c) Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE) d) Quantum
Approximate Optimization Algorithm (QAOA)

The correct answer is c) Variational Quantum Eigensolver (VQE).

9. The “no-cloning theorem” in quantum mechanics implies that: a) Qubits cannot

be measured without disturbing their state. b) An arbitrary unknown quantum

state cannot be perfectly copied. c) Entanglement cannot be created between

distant qubits. d) Quantum computers cannot simulate classical computers.

The correct answer is b) An arbitrary unknown quantum state cannot be perfectly copied.

10. Which of these PQC approaches relies on the difficulty of problems in

mathematical lattices? a) Hash-based cryptography b) Code-based cryptography

c) Multivariate polynomial cryptography d) Lattice-based cryptography

The correct answer is d) Lattice-based cryptography.
